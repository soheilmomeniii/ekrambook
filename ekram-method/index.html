Front Matter — F1

Preface

AI made building cheap. It made being understood expensive. For the first time, a single founder can ship what once took a team of fifty. I know because I am doing it, and so is everyone around me. Every barrier that falls lets in a thousand more builders, and the tools that made you fast made everyone fast. Millions of new products are coming, many of them good, built by founders who executed well. Most will disappear without ever being misunderstood, because they were never understood in the first place. That is the trade the AI era made on your behalf. Execution used to be the scarce edge. Now the scarce edge is meaning: whether a market can say what you are, want it, and believe it is where the world is going. Positioning stopped being a marketing tactic layered on at the end and became the strategic foundation that decides how a company is understood from day one. When I became a founder, I felt this directly. Growth tactics were not enough. Posting was not enough. I needed to understand, from first principles, why some ideas capture a market's imagination while others vanish. The clearest thinking I ever found on that question came from Ekram. He had already mapped the perception layer: how positions form, how categories get claimed, how belief compounds into inevitability. But his map reached me scattered, a four-word tweet here, a keynote there, essays, interviews, replies. Fragments are hard to learn from and easy to lose, and nobody had brought them together. This book is my attempt. I collected the public record, verified every line, and arranged it into the method I wish someone had handed me on day one. The words in the gray bands are his. The arrangement, and any mistakes in it, are mine. One thing this book is not: a book for Ekram's audience. His work grew out of crypto, the market where perception moves fastest and getting it wrong is fatal soonest. The method travels. If you are building in the AI era, this map was drawn under harsher weather than yours, and it reads like it was drawn for you. If you can build, and soon everyone can, this is the discipline that decides whether what you build matters. Somo, 2026

Reading Key

Five kinds of text, never blended.

  • Exact words. Verbatim tweets and blog captures, in quotation marks, with a source line and a URL. Wording is exact. Punctuation is normalized to plain ASCII, straight quotes, no em dashes. Where the book quotes part of a longer post, the source line says (segment); the link always opens the full post, so the complete context is one tap away.
  • Edited transcript excerpt. Lines spoken in a talk, lightly cleaned for reading. Labeled as such, set without quotation marks, and never treated as verbatim unless the wording is confirmed unchanged.
  • Synthesis. The book's framing and method language, in the arranger's voice. Never put in Ekram's mouth.
  • Inherited. The canon he builds on: Ries and Trout, Cunningham, Thiel, Bezos, Drucker, Sacks.
  • Case. Public events, public facts, with the reading labeled as ours. The gray bands throughout this book carry only the first two kinds: his exact words, or his labeled transcript lines. Skim the bands and you are reading Ekram, nobody else. One case, in Chapter 2, is drawn from outside his corpus; an editor's note there says so.

Movement

Meaning

What are you, and why should the market understand you? Meaning is the floor. Before a market can want you or believe you, it has to file you. This movement is the rational anchor: decide what you are, refuse what you are not, and compress it into something the market can carry. Miss it and nothing above stands. A vivid brand on a confused position only makes the confusion louder.

Chapter One

Position Or Be Positioned

1
"position, or be positioned."
Ekram , 2017. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/888465119717408768

Four words, posted in 2017, years before the essays and the keynotes. Everything else in this book is that sentence paying its costs. A position is the place you occupy in someone else's mind. You do not fully control it, and you cannot opt out of having one. The market files you the moment it notices you, using the nearest cached label, and whoever wrote that label owns your first impression. Ekram's essays return to this without mercy:

"the act of positioning happens whether we like it or not. Competitors slander us. Partners invent our script. Even customers have the potential of spreading narratives that are disadvantageous to us."
Ekram , essay "Anchor the market," ekram.xyz, 2022.

Notice who does the positioning in those three sentences. Competitors. Partners. Customers. Everyone except you. The sentence about your company is getting written today, by people who owe you no effort and will not check their work. In the keynote he collapses it to a fork with no third option:

If you do not position, you will be positioned. If you don't choose a category, you will be assigned a category.
Ekram , positioning keynote. Edited transcript excerpt.

Watch it happen to a well-funded thesis. Worldcoin arrived with a serious claim: proof of personhood for the age of AI, a way to tell humans from machines online. The public conversation never filed the thesis. It filed the orb. An iris-scanning sphere is a more vivid sentence than a personhood protocol, so the image did the positioning, the privacy question rode along with it, and governments in several countries paused the project or opened investigations. Ekram was skeptical of the self-description the project led with:

"Idk about this 'verified humans.'"
Ekram , 2025. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1917795103994761670

The lesson is uncomfortable precisely because Worldcoin had done the hard part. The technology was real. The thesis was coherent. And the market's lazy sentence beat both to the filing cabinet. (The Worldcoin reading is ours, a Tier 2 case; his line above is the extent of what he said.) So the first exercise in the method is an act of humility. Before you write the position you want, write the one you will get by default. Say the lazy sentence out loud, the one a distracted stranger would produce four seconds after seeing your homepage. If you can live with that sentence, you may not need to fight. If you cannot, you now know exactly which perception you are trying to overwrite, and every chapter that follows is a tool for overwriting it. If you do not position yourself, you have still been positioned. You just did not get a say.

Chapter Two

Every Company Exists Twice

2

T H E G A P I S T H E J O B · Z E R O G A P F I G . 0 1 · C H 2 T H E G A P = T H E J O B Y O U A R E T H E Y S A Y G A P = 0 C L O S E D F R O M T H E S T O R Y S I D TWO SENTENCES SIT APART; THE WHOLE JOB IS CLOSING THE GAP, FROM THE STORY SIDE.

"Every company exists twice: once as reality, and once as the story people tell about it"
Ekram , 2026. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/2067652948206010490

August 2020. A team copied Uniswap almost line for line, named the copy SushiSwap, and ran a vampire attack to drain its liquidity. For a few weeks it worked; more than a billion dollars of liquidity migrated. Code, incentives, interface: all successfully forked. Then the market chose. Uniswap stayed the default, the name people reached for, the standard other projects compared themselves against. The fork kept the code and lost the argument. Ekram's four-word law explains the outcome:

"You can't fork brand."
Ekram , 2024. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1870995210161029238

What happened is the doctrine of this chapter. Every company exists twice. Once as reality: the code, the team, the treasury, the thing itself. Once as story: what the market believes the thing is. SushiSwap forked the first existence perfectly and could not touch the second, because the second lives in other people's heads, and heads do not accept pull requests. Capital, talent, and press never see your reality. They see the story, and they act on the story. An investor funds the story. A hire joins the story. A journalist repeats the story. The reality determines whether they stay, but the story determines whether they arrive. Control the second existence or it controls the first. Counter-case: General Magic. (Tier 2, editor case.) The opposite failure is a real product whose story never forms. In 1994 a team spun out of Apple shipped the future: a pocket device for messages, calls, and commerce over a network, built by people who would go on to create the iPod, the iPhone's core, Android, and eBay. The engineering was real and the vision was right, thirteen years early. What never arrived was the sentence. A phone that was also a computer had no file to live in, "personal communicator" named a category nobody was carrying, and the market that met the device could not say what it was or why now. It sold almost nothing and faded within a few years. The polite history says too early. The sharper reading is that too early is what a market calls a future nobody made legible: when the same idea returned in 2007, it arrived as three words everyone already understood, a phone, an iPod, an internet communicator, and it swallowed the world. Same future. Different sentence. Invisibility, the failure state of good engineering with no author. The working move: write what the company is in one sentence. Write what the market says it is in one sentence. The gap between those two sentences is the whole job, and the rest of this book is a method for closing it from the story side. The market never meets your product. It meets the story of your product, and it decides before it checks. Source. Uniswap and SushiSwap (2020) and General Magic (1994-2002) are public record; both readings are ours. General Magic appears nowhere in Ekram's corpus, as the note above says. The 2007 three-words framing is Steve Jobs's, from the iPhone introduction, paraphrased.

Chapter Three

Position First, Then Brand

3

T H E L I S T F I G . 0 2 · C H 3 01 W H A T I S I T ? THE SHELF THE MIND FILES YOU ON 02 W H Y D O E S I T M A T T E R ? RELEVANCE — WHERE POSITIONING DIES 03 W H Y Y O U , N O T A S U B S T I T U T E ? THE ONE THING A FORK CAN'T CARRY 04 W H Y N O W ? WHAT MAKES YOU INEVITABLE, NOT OPTIONAL THE FOUR QUESTIONS: THE RATIONAL SKELETON EVERY BRAND IS BUILT ON.

"Marketing is the art of positioning."
Ekram , essay "Start with Positioning, not Branding," ekram.xyz.

Principle. Positioning is the claim you author: what you are, who for, why it matters, why now. Brand is the response that claim earns. You control one. You only influence the other. As Bezos put it and Ekram repeats, a brand is what people say when you are not in the room. The order is fixed, and getting it backwards is the most common expensive mistake in frontier marketing. In his words. He frames the pair as rational and emotional:

Positioning is rational, branding is emotional. You need both. You position first before you build a brand.
Ekram , positioning keynote. Edited transcript excerpt.

A feeling has to be a feeling about something. Position first, or you are painting a portrait before deciding who is in it. Case: Celestia. (Tier 1, essay and keynote.) The positioning statement came first, flat and ownable:

"First modular blockchain network to power scalable, secure Web3 apps"
Celestia positioning statement, quoted by Ekram in the essay examples , ekram.xyz.

One word, "modular," that the market could carry and repeat. The brand came after, and from the culture rather than an agency. Its rallying line surfaced from the team itself, a story Chapter 10 tells in full. He signs the phrase himself:

"be different, build whatever"
Ekram , 2024. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1861525667340525970

Position first, the word the market owns. Brand second, the feeling that grows on it. The mistake. Treating a tagline as a position. The claim underneath a tagline is what a reporter would print with the hype stripped out. Ship the feeling without the claim and the market has nothing to file. Method. Answer what it is, why it matters, why now, and why you, before you touch voice, color, or archetype. If any answer reaches for an adjective, it is not an answer yet. Source. "Position first, then brand" is Cunningham's; the not-in-the-room line is Bezos's, repeated by Ekram. The rational-emotional split is an edited transcript excerpt.

Chapter Four

Sacrifice Is The Price Of Meaning

4

His words carry this chapter; the arranger only holds the door.

"One of the lessons a mentor (who worked with Steve Jobs to launch the Macintosh) taught me: A great positioning strategy must hurt. The act is inherently an act of sacrifice. It should feel like you're giving something up. And that requires pain."
Ekram , 2026 (segment). https://x.com/ekrahm/status/2059011134146179424

The mentor is Andy Cunningham; the hard edge is hers, and he keeps it sharp. A position is made by subtraction. A claim that fits everyone describes no one.

"You are either a wandering generality or a meaningful specific."
Ekram , essay "Sacrifice to find your Position," ekram.xyz, 2022.

The wandering generality is the default state of every ambitious company: capable of many things, therefore claiming many things, therefore filed as nothing. The exit is a cut that costs real money or a real audience.

"Every "no" is a deeper "yes" towards the audience that matters the most."
Ekram , essay "Sacrifice to find your Position," ekram.xyz, 2022.

In the keynote he runs the discipline through Solana: refuse the broad ground, take one axis, speed, and work until the market ranks the whole layer-one category by it. The sacrifice underneath is choosing who to ignore. (Edited transcript excerpt.) He reads the same discipline in Solana's sub-brands:

"Solana is good at finding derivatives to underscore their strategic position. Breakpoint, Lightspeed, and now Blink. These brands are all saying the same thing: "speed kills"."
Ekram , 2024. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1805630020431519809

One warning, and one test. The warning: a list of no's that costs nothing is a description, and any rival can hold it too. The test: list what the company will stop being, name the audience you are not for, say it out loud. If the list does not sting, cut deeper. The wince is the signal. Source. The Law of Sacrifice is Ries and Trout. The must-hurt edge is Cunningham: the tweet above credits an unnamed mentor, and Ekram names her in the keynote. The Solana narration is edited transcript.

Chapter Five

Make The Position Fit In The Mind

5

T H E I C E B E R G F I G . 0 3 · C H 5 THE TIP · WHAT YOU SAY WATERLINE THE FULL MASS REVEALED OVER TIME CLAIM DEPTH COMMUNICATE ONLY THE TIP. REVEAL THE MASS AS YOU EARN THE RIGHT TO WIDEN.

"The name is the thesis."
Ekram , 2026 (segment). https://x.com/ekrahm/status/2069484019264491658

A position has to travel through distracted minds, so it has to be small enough to carry. Naming and language are compression, and this chapter is the compression toolkit. The four-way test. Run every name, phrase, and line of launch copy through one question: does it explain, compress, provoke, or merely decorate. Keep the first three. Kill the fourth. The name test. When a project called Engram crossed his feed, his whole read of the name was the method in one move:

"clever name. In neuroscience, an engram is the physical trace of a memory stored in the brain. The name is the thesis."
Ekram , 2026. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/2069484019264491658

The name does the positioning work before a single feature is explained. Ask what future your name tells the market to expect. When a product line's names sprawl instead, the market cannot build a ladder of what beats what:

"OpenAI naming committee has seen things"
Ekram , 2026. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/2070567702557765691

Statement, then tagline. In the keynote he separates the two cleanly. "No Software" for Salesforce, "One Click" for Amazon, "only possible on Solana": taglines. A positioning statement is the matterof-fact articulation of who you are and why you matter; the tagline is the emotional one-liner that expresses it. Ship the tagline while skipping the statement and you have a mood with nothing under it. (Edited transcript excerpt.) Dead language. Most launch copy is packing material the market is trained to skip:

"The whole "we're excited to announce..." opening is SO overdone"
Ekram , 2024. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1770937126646931499

Cut every phrase that only sounds like a launch. What survives the cut is your actual language: the words doing explain, compress, or provoke work. There will be fewer of them than you wrote. Good. Source. The statement-versus-tagline teaching is an edited transcript excerpt from the keynote.

Category

What game are you making the market play? you are filed on. You have three moves: create a new category, enter an existing one and win an axis, or make the leader's category feel old. Category work is where positioning turns into strategy, because it sets the comparison the market judges you by. Get it right and the comparison flatters you. Get it wrong and you spend years arguing on someone else's terms.

Chapter Six

Create The Category Or Enter The Fight

6

T H E E X I T S F I G . 0 4 · C H 6 S U C C E S S C O M P E T E C R E A T E T H E O N L Y W A Y O U T I S U P T H E M U D D L E D M I D D L E BOTH ENDS WIN; THE BOTTOM IS WHERE THE COMPANY FLATTENS OUT.

"everyone wants to create a new market category.few have the will to define and teach it. even fewer have the discipline to stay on message."
Ekram , 2024. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1806044294308122990

Principle. Three moves, three bills. Create a category and you own a word, but you carry the burden of education. Enter an existing category and you inherit its demand, but you fight on a visible axis forever. Position against the leader and you borrow their attention, but you live in their shadow until you break out. The one move that never works is the straddle: "new and better" is two stories, and the market holds neither. In his words. Ekram frames the fork through David Sacks' two formulas: The Jordan Way is to compete and win, and to double down when the competition gets fierce. The Thiel Way is to avoid competition and create your own game.

The middle is where most brands die. There is a graveyard of teams who chose the Thiel path and could not carry the burden of education.
Ekram , positioning keynote. Edited transcript excerpt. (Jordan vers us Thiel is David Sacks , cited by Ekram ; the differentiation modes are Andy Cunningham .)

Case: same market, two games. In the keynote, Ekram runs both moves through the layer-one market. Solana played Jordan: it stayed inside the category, declared speed the axis that matters, and worked to make the market rank everyone by it. Celestia played Thiel: it refused the race and created a new category, modular, so the old comparison stopped applying. Same market, two legitimate games, neither straddling. The Jordan bill is proof and defense, because the moment you win an axis, rivals copy it. The Thiel bill is patience, because a new word means nobody understands you yet. Method. Say the word out loud: Jordan or Thiel. Name the axis you will win on, or the category you will define. Then price the bill. If you cannot pay for proof and defense, do not pick Jordan. If you cannot teach for a year, do not pick Thiel. And keep the endgame in view:

"Eventually, every category becomes a two-car race"
Ekram , 2022. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1558271694283816960

Source. Jordan versus Thiel is David Sacks, cited by Ekram; the three differentiation modes are Cunningham. Both come from the keynote.

Bangkok, November 2024. One post from Ekram lists the week's gatherings, and the category is baked into the names: Modular Bangkok. Modular Brunch. Modular Acceleration. Provable Bangkok. A wall of events, each one putting the same word in front of the ecosystem again. Nothing about that list is an accident. Celestia had coined "modular" years earlier, and coining the word was the easy part. A category only becomes real when other projects, media, and even competitors start using its language. Until then it is a claim one company makes about itself, and the market distrusts a market of one. So the work after the word is teaching: define the problem, hand the language to others, and repeat it until the market says it back without you. The hardest part of that teaching is generosity toward rivals. From the keynote, on the modular narrative:

The modular narrative is only as good as its inclusiveness. It is important for other projects to identify as modular. There is no such thing as a category of one, so you make sure other projects feel comfortable identifying as modular and have the resources to build in it.
Ekram , positioning keynote. Edited transcript excerpt.

Read the Bangkok list again with that in mind. Every event is the category being taught. The rivals who show up become proof that the category is bigger than one project, which is the entire point. Fence the word off and it stays yours and stays small. Give it away and it becomes the board everyone plays on, with your company standing at the door. The method follows the case. Name the category. Define the problem it solves in one sentence. Recruit other players into it, including rivals, and give them the language and the resources to adopt it. Then teach on a loop until the market repeats it without crediting you. The day a competitor calls themselves by your category name, you have won. Source. The inclusiveness passage is edited transcript; the event names are described from the linked tweet, not quoted. The reading of the list is ours.

Chapter Eight

Make The Competitor Feel Historical

8
"The best way to kill your competitor is to make them feel historical. I meet a lot of founders who refuse to antagonize their main competitor."
Ekram , 2026 (segment). https://x.com/ekrahm/status/2059706055291232673

Superiority is an argument, and the market has to check arguments. A frame skips the check. Name an old world and a new world, stand at the door of the new one, and the leader gets filed as the past before anyone examines a feature. "There's a saying in politics that the strongest way to unite a people is through a common enemy. I've found the same principle applies effectively in marketing and branding."
Ekram , 2024. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1823441724599075044
"Antagonists to the brand create urgency and relevance. Strangely, 98% of founders I meet are too soft to choose one."
Ekram , 2024. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1804530184646791577

In the keynote he borrows the mechanic from political communication: anchor the audience onto a single word that engineers a bias against the rival, the way a campaign brands an opponent with one sticky label. You are not calling the competitor bad. You are choosing the lens the market reads them through. (Edited transcript excerpt.) Pick the fight carefully, though. Some prizes shrink while you win them:

"Being #1 in a shrinking market isn't a flex. Play stupid games. Win stupid prizes."
Ekram , 2025. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1920530990184771936

The same move works even when the enemy is not a named competitor. Sometimes the rival is a frame: the old debate, the accusation, the category the market is trying to trap you inside. The job then changes from dunking on a company to making the frame itself feel too small for the future now arriving. Call it the historical move at category scale: stop defending yourself, and change what the argument is about. A later moment shows the move run outside a direct rivalry. When Anthropic found itself at the center of a public fight, Ekram's read of its statement was one line:

"The most interesting line in Anthropic's statement is the one where the debate stops being about Anthropic. Turn your case into a category question."
Ekram , 2026. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/2065631212643975402

The accusation was the frame. Anthropic's statement made that frame feel obsolete. One warning. Antagonism that is only an insult reads as insecurity and ages badly. The frame has to point at a real shift, an old world that is actually ending, or it is noise aimed at a rival. Name the old world, the new world, and the reason the old leader cannot cross over without betraying what made them. Then let the market carry the comparison. The market inherits a frame before it fact-checks one. Source. The frame-control mechanic is from the positioning keynote, used here as an edited transcript excerpt. The Anthropic read comes from his linked tweet; framing it as the historical move at category scale is ours.

Chapter Nine

Brand Is A Response You Earn

9
"It's a response to your positioning, not a stimulus."
Ekram , essay "Start with Positioning, not Branding," ekram.xyz.

Principle. You do not ship a brand. You send signals: a message, a product, a launch. Whatever sticks in other people's heads is the brand. The only levers you hold are your position and your behavior; the brand is the output. Which is why "let's build a brand" is the wrong first move, and why a feeling has to be a feeling about something. In his words. In the keynote he traces the word back to cattle ranching, a mark burned to tell animals apart, then lands the point:

A brand is a prize. It is a response you earn. You send a signal, you stimulate the market, some of it lands and some of it doesn't, and whatever lands chisels a perception. That perception is the brand.
Ekram , positioning keynote. Edited transcript excerpt.

The diagnostic: no FUD, no brand. If the market feels nothing about you, it will not bother to attack you, and indifference is the real failure state.

"The most distinct sign of irrelevance is no-FUD. If no one is trying to undermine, criticize, or cast doubt on you, it's a stark indicator that you aren't important enough to be worth attacking."
Ekram , 2025. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1889415909548462352
"Be very worried about the absence of attacks on the brand. Means ppl don't give a shit"
Ekram , 2024. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1823196501243473933

Case: a response, witnessed. (Tier 1, his own read.) Watch him catch the moment a brand comes back from the market:

"Succinct's brand personality seems to really be shining through lately. Unique blend of spontaneity, wit and power."
Ekram , 2025. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1889502494784627142

Note what he is describing. Nobody at Succinct shipped "spontaneity, wit and power" as a deliverable. They sent signals; the market returned a personality, and an outside strategist could name the feeling without being prompted. A brand exists on the day someone else can describe it. The mistake. Diluting the signal until no response can form:

"Using 'partnership' in every announcement dilutes the brand. The word is so overused it has lost its meaning."
Ekram , 2024. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1801438746988163375

Method. Name the one emotional response you want repeated without prompting. Audit your last month of signals, the product, the copy, the launches, and ask what feeling they actually earned. If the honest answer is "none," you do not have a brand yet. You have a position waiting for one. Source. The prize-and-response passage is edited transcript. The Succinct line is his; the interpretation is ours.

Chapter Eleven

The Founder Is The Lightning Rod

11

T H E W E L L F I G . 0 8 · C H 1 1 M E A N I N G A T T E N T I O N T A L E N T C A P I T A L T H E F O U N D E R T H E F O U N D E R MASS BENDS THE FIELD: MEANING, ATTENTION, TALENT, CAPITAL ROLL TOWARD THE FOUNDER.

"The founder is the lightning rod to the brand"
Ekram , 2025. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1879257403390394622

Early markets cannot evaluate companies. The product is unfinished, the metrics are noise, the claims all sound alike. So the market reads the founder instead: their taste, their risk appetite, their conviction, and it prices the company off the person. One human becomes the compression point for everything the company means, whether anyone planned it or not. Ekram watches this mechanism the way a meteorologist watches pressure systems. He has named its stages. There is the debut, where status is borrowed before it is earned:

"some of the fastest founder visibility ascents I've seen start with triangulation as the debut move. basically showing up next to two other founders who already have the status they want."
Ekram , 2026 (segment). https://x.com/ekrahm/status/2013779817435668526

There is the amplification layer, which the founder does not control alone:

"People look at your inner-circle, your disciples. If they're silent or mid, it dims the brand. But if they're sharp, vocal, and credible, it creates pull."
Ekram , 2025 (segment). https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1915520650674135110

And there is the terminal stage, where the person stops being a communicator and becomes a symbol. Ekram's live example was Roy Lee, the founder of the AI startup Cluely, whose public provocations had become a bigger story than his product:

"What we're watching is the early formation of a cult-ofpersonality. Roy is becoming the Gen Z symbol of defiance, embodying what many founders secretly wish they had the courage to say."
Ekram , 2026 (segment). https://x.com/ekrahm/status/2031200935314338160

At full charge, the market stops buying the product and buys what the founder represents instead. Ekram calls the posture main character mode, the marketing analogue to Paul Graham's founder mode (" 'Main character mode' is the marketing analogue to PGs Founder mode," 2024). The physics cut both ways, and this caution is ours rather than his. The same concentration that makes belief compound also concentrates blame. A founder who becomes the symbol of the upside becomes the symbol of the collapse if it comes. The rod attracts the strike as well as the charge, and that is the bargain you accept when you put a human at the center of the brand. So the decision has three parts, made on purpose rather than by drift. Find the founder's authentic edge and the one visible, costly risk they will take in public, because the market prices risk taken, not words. Curate the people who echo them, because the signal compounds through the inner circle or dims there. And decide whether the brand should wear a face at all, knowing what the face will absorb in both directions. A market that cannot read your company will read your founder. Choose what it finds. The rod-cuts-both-ways caution is ours, with no named case.

"If the war is attention, narrative is the frontline"
Ekram , 2024. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1864023288999031153

A narrative is the future the company makes more believable, and in frontier markets that future often arrives before the product does:

"In crypto, narrative market-fit often precedes product marketfit"
Ekram , 2022. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1536782146265788417

Belief organizes the market before there is anything to point at. Which raises the question of how you tell a real narrative from a decorated slogan. He has a test:

Chapter Thirteen

Sound The Alarm

13
"One comms strategy is to outright create the crisis. Signal warning. Unite tribes. Articulate ramifications. Channel that alarm into market action."
Ekram , 2023. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1616251011982823424

November 2024. Ethereum researcher Justin Drake announces Beam Chain, a proposal to renew Ethereum's consensus layer. A real problem, a serious researcher, a major stage. And Ekram scores it as a failure:

"It was a communications blunder"
Ekram , 2024 (segment). https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1856582705045278824

His diagnosis, from the same post, came down to two phrases: "Poor problem framing" and a "prolonged timeline." The existing chain was cast as old and frozen, which supplies the danger. But the fix was years away, so the danger had no deadline, and an alarm with no near-term cost never converts into action. The announcement produced discourse instead of urgency. A danger with no clock is an announcement, and the market filed it as one. The doctrine hiding in that failure: strong positioning often names a danger, because the market moves when the cost of doing nothing becomes visible. But alarm and hype are different instruments. Hype inflates a reward that may never arrive. Alarm makes a real, already-moving downside legible. What separates them is whether a genuine shift sits underneath, and whether the deadline is close enough to bite. Beam Chain had the shift and lost the deadline. The sharpest operators compress the choice into a fork with no middle. Ekram studied the pattern:

"Been studying the power of binary outcomes in brand narrative lately."
Ekram , 2024 (segment). https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1849912273042854389

In that post he reads Elon's companies as binaries: SpaceX as Mars or extinction, Tesla as sustainable energy or climate crisis, Neuralink as AI or obsolescence. Name the fork and inaction starts to feel like choosing the bad branch. The mechanic is portable; the honesty requirement travels with it. The method, learned from the failure: name what happens if the market ignores the shift, and make the deadline close enough to bite. If there is no real downside and no near-term cost, you are running hype, and the market eventually prices the difference. Source. The Beam Chain diagnosis phrases are verbatim segments of his linked post; the Elon binaries are described from his post. The urgency-versus-hype distinction is ours.

Win Attention, Then Defend It

15

Ekram , 2024. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1800730344674701554

Attention is the scarce input, and winning the glance is only the start. The work runs in three moves: create it, put it where it lives, defend it when the story turns. Run them as a checklist. Create it. The content that travels is a hard idea made shareable without losing the insight:

"Often what spreads the most in crypto is when you take a high IQ concept and meme it into more approachable forms."

And it is earned, never bought:

"Like respect, attention is earned"
Ekram , 2022. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1570076419719581703

Name the attention source and how you earn it. Then name the proof that has to follow, or the attention decays into a memory of a glance. Channel it. The channels move under your feet, so check where attention actually lives before you spend on it:

"Streaming has overtaken podcasting as crypto's dominant media format."
Ekram , 2025 (segment). https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1966590732745273451

Defend it. The moment the story turns, control goes to whoever speaks first and fullest. His crisis spine is one line:

"In other words, he or she who discloses first, wins."
Ekram , essay "Issue Full Dis closure," ekram.xyz.

The second move is the reframe, and he named its mechanism years before most founders needed it:

"In crisis-communications, identify a "pivot-point", shifting attention from A to B"
Ekram , 2022. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1565912551681957888

Case: Euler. (Tier 2, public record; his line below is the extent of what he said.) In March 2023 the lending protocol Euler suffered one of DeFi's largest hacks, roughly two hundred million dollars drained in a morning. The team disclosed immediately, negotiated with the attacker in public, and recovered the funds within weeks. Then it made the defense permanent: it kept the name. No rebrand, no distancing, the history owned in full. Ekram read the choice as the relaunch approached:

"Euler keeping the same name is interesting. Sets them up for a comeback narrative. Respect."
Ekram , 2024. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1832155880575660160

A rebrand would have spent the crisis. The kept name invested it: the protocol relaunched under the same flag, and the hack became the first chapter of a comeback story instead of the last chapter of a failure. The crisis page, written before you need it. Disclose first, fast, and full. Acknowledge plainly, no euphemism. Find the pivotpoint and shift the story from the accusation to the answer. Decide today who reviews the response before it posts, because you will not have time to decide it during. Source. The create-channel-defend frame is ours. Chapter Sixteen

The Positioning Machine

16

Celestia, the method made visible. This book runs in parts because that is how you learn it. It ships as one machine. Here is the whole thing turning at once, in a company that ran every stage of it in public. Meaning. Celestia could have arrived as one more layerone and been filed as one more layer-one. It refused the default and built the position on a word the market could own, flat and factual and repeatable:

"First modular blockchain network to power scalable, secure Web3 apps"
Celestia positioning statement, quoted by Ekram in the essay examples , ekram.xyz.

Modular was the position. The feeling came later. The word came first. Category. Modular was not a feature; it was a new category, the Thiel move, and a category of one is a category of none. So Celestia did the expensive part and taught it, pulling rivals in rather than fencing the word off:

"coordinating with rivals makes the category inevitable"
Ekram , 2026. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/2036164302659526939

The teaching was made physical: the run of gatherings with the category baked into their names, until the word sat in front of the ecosystem more often than any argument for it. "be different, build whatever"
Ekram , 2024. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1861525667340525970

Position first, the word the market owns. Brand second, the feeling the tribe wears. Inevitability. Because the story arrived before the product was finished, it organized builders on belief, which is how frontier markets often move:

"In crypto, narrative market-fit often precedes product marketfit"
Ekram , 2022. https://x.com/ekrahm/status/1536782146265788417

Modular held long enough to enter the market's language. That is the real win. The word stopped belonging only to Celestia and became a way the market described a whole architecture, a whole debate, a whole board. People could argue for modular, against modular, or build inside modular, but they were now using the frame. Delivery. The category entered the market the same way it was taught: events, ecosystem teaching, and adopters taking the modular language as their own. Each adoption proved the category was larger than one team. The list, again, was the positioning. The machine. Run the stages back to back and the seams disappear. The position set the category. The category needed teaching, so the culture and the events carried it. The culture produced a brand the tribe wanted to wear. The narrative held long enough to become market language, and delivery fed the whole loop back to meaning. Five movements, one perception, authored on purpose and compounded through every stage. The payoff is not a tagline. The payoff is who shows up:

"One of the coolest aspects of Celestia is the caliber of builders one gets to meet. Always impressed by the sheer scope of ambition and drive to build global brands. Life is too short to be around anyone else."

The boundary. The honest lesson is sharper than the victory lap. Modular entering the language proved what positioning can do, and only that. A word can win the board while the company behind it still competes for builders, liquidity, attention, and time. Reality still collects: product, execution, timing. Chapter 2 named this law before the machine ever turned: the story is the existence the market meets first, and reality is the one that decides whether anyone stays. The machine authors the first existence. The second one is earned. Story creates the board. Reality decides how long you keep your seat. Source. F. Toolkits

8 6 An operator's kit, organized by the decision you are facing. Working tools, not homework. Where a tool is the book's own procedure it is marked editor synthesis; where it rests on the keynote it is marked edited transcript. The doctrine behind each tool is sourced in the chapters and the quote bank. When you are pos itioning E DI TE D TRANSCRI PT

→ What is it. the noun a reporter would print with the marketing stripped out. → Why does it matter. the value, not the feature; ask 'so what' until you hit something a human wants. → Why you, not a substitute. the real alternative is usually the status quo. → Why now. the trend that makes this the moment. We are the [what] that [why it matters], for people who [who], unlike [the substitute], and now is the moment because [why now]. If a blank makes you reach for an adjective, you have not answered it. Two checks: the reporter test, strip every emotional word and see if a claim remains; the empty-room test, would your best customer say this sentence to a peer when you are not there.

8 7 When you are s acrificing E DI TOR SYNTH E SI S

→ The one word or space you are trying to own. → What you must stop claiming: every other thing your homepage says you are, cut to one. → Who you are not for, named out loud. → The no you keep dodging because it costs real money or a real segment. If none of the four made you wince, cut deeper.

When you are choos ing the game E DI TOR SYNTH E SI S

→ Say the word: compete or create, no straddle. → Name what you own: the axis you win on, or the category you define. → Name the comparison you accept or escape. → Price the bill: proof and defense for Jordan, education and patience for Thiel.

When you are naming E DI TOR SYNTH E SI S

→ Does the name or phrase explain, compress, provoke, or merely decorate. → What future does the name tell the market to expect. → Cut dead launch language, starting with "we're excited to announce."

8 8 When a competitor is winning E DI TOR SYNTH E SI S · CH 8

→ Do not argue superiority; the market has to check arguments. → Name the old world, the new world, and why the leader cannot cross without betraying what made them. → Choose the enemy deliberately: a rival, the status quo, or the whole category. → Check the prize first: if the market is shrinking, decline the fight. If the attack is on you, turn your case into a category question.

When you are building the narrative E DI TOR SYNTH E SI S

→ Write the future that recedes if the company disappears, in one sentence. → Test consistency: one thing said many ways and held for years, or a story that resets every six months.

8 9 When nobody feels the urgency E DI TOR SYNTH E SI S · CH 13

→ Name what happens if the market ignores the shift, in one sentence. → Put the deadline close enough to bite; a danger with no clock is an announcement. → Run the honesty test: alarm makes a real, near-term downside visible, hype inflates a reward that may never arrive. → When the shift is real, frame the binary so inaction feels like choosing the bad branch.

When you are launching E DI TOR SYNTH E SI S

→ Decide what the launch must prove before what it says: inevitability, momentum, a coalition, a direction. → Stage the proof so the market reads it first. → Check the timing: do not ship into a bigger story.
